Explore our comprehensive digital repository of human skeletal remains

Collection Overview

The UCLA 3D Osteology Collection features high-resolution 3D models of human skeletal remains, organized by anatomical region. Each model has been meticulously scanned using advanced 3D scanning technology to ensure accurate representation of anatomical features. Our collection serves as a valuable resource for researchers, educators, and students in the fields of anthropology, anatomy, and forensic science.

All models in our collection have been created with strict adherence to ethical guidelines and NAGPRA regulations. Our digital approach allows for detailed study without physical handling of remains, supporting both research needs and cultural respect.

Using Our Collection

  • For Researchers
  • For Educators
  • For Students
  • For Tribal Representatives

For Researchers

Our collection provides valuable resources for researchers in anthropology, anatomy, forensic science, and related fields. Benefits include:

  • High-resolution 3D models with accurate anatomical details
  • Ability to take precise measurements using digital tools
  • Access to rare or fragile specimens that may be difficult to study physically
  • Ethical alternative to handling physical remains
  • Standardized format for comparative studies

Researchers interested in using our models for publication should review our usage guidelines and proper citation format.

For Educators

Our 3D models offer excellent teaching resources for courses in anatomy, anthropology, and related fields:

  • Interactive visual aids for classroom instruction
  • Downloadable models for use in educational presentations
  • Consistent specimens for standardized teaching
  • Ethical alternative to using physical specimens in teaching
  • Ability to highlight specific anatomical features

We offer educational resource packages specifically designed for classroom use. Contact us for more information about educational licensing.

For Students

Students can benefit from our collection in numerous ways:

  • Study anatomical features from any angle and at any scale
  • Practice identification and measurement skills
  • Access high-quality specimens regardless of institutional resources
  • Supplement traditional textbook learning with interactive 3D models
  • Understand anatomical variation through comparative study

Students are encouraged to explore our learning modules designed to guide educational use of the collection.

For Tribal Representatives

We recognize the cultural and spiritual significance of human remains to descendant communities:

  • Our digital collection supports repatriation efforts by preserving research access while allowing physical remains to be returned
  • We adhere strictly to NAGPRA and CalNAGPRA regulations
  • Access to certain models may be restricted based on cultural sensitivities
  • We welcome consultation with tribal representatives regarding the presentation and use of our models

Tribal representatives with questions or concerns about our collection are encouraged to contact us directly.

Access and Usage

Our collection is available through different access levels depending on the intended use:

Public Access

Selected models are available for public viewing through our online viewer. These models can be rotated and examined but cannot be downloaded or measured.

Educational Access

Educators and students can request access to our educational collection, which includes downloadable models and measurement tools for classroom use.

Research Access

Qualified researchers can apply for full access to our collection, including high-resolution models and advanced measurement tools for research purposes.

To request access beyond the public level, please complete our access request form.
