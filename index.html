About the Collection

Welcome to UCLA's digital repository of 3D bone scans, a project focused on ethical, non-destructive study of human skeletal remains. Through cutting-edge 3D scanning technologies, this project provides high-resolution models that allow researchers to explore bones without physical handling.

This platform supports not only detailed analysis but also aims to aid in the repatriation of remains to their rightful communities, honoring their cultural and ancestral connections. Additionally, the precise data collected here is instrumental in developing innovative methods for skeletal re-articulation and pair-matching, enabling accurate reconstructions and improved identification practices.

The goal is to advance ethical standards in anthropology, bioarchaeology, and forensic science while fostering respect and accountability.

Ethical Framework

Our project is deeply rooted in ethical innovation, seeking to promote respect for cultural and spiritual values by reducing physical handling of remains and creating detailed digital and physical replicas.

One of our primary goals is to support the potential repatriation of remains by creating accurate, non-destructive records. Through 3D scanning, we can document and study human remains in a way that facilitates their respectful return to descendant communities when appropriate.

Dignity of the Dead +

The ethical treatment of human remains is a foundational principle in osteological research. Human remains deserve a particular moral status that extends beyond their biological state.

NAGPRA Compliance +

We strictly adhere to the Native American Graves Protection and Repatriation Act (NAGPRA) of 1990 and the California Native American Graves Protection and Repatriation Act (CalNAGPRA).

Non-Invasive Documentation +

Our project employs 3D scanning technology as an ethical alternative to traditional invasive research methods, minimizing physical handling of remains.
